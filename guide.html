Patriots RV Services — Employee Guide

Every morning the dashboard emails out a report — the Manager Daily Report and the Parts Report. Those reports flag what needs your attention today. This page explains what each flag means and exactly what to do about it.

Reports will link straight here. When a report says “For more detailed guidance, click here”, it drops you at the matching section below.

Start here

Everyone

We are moving to an AI-assisted setup where the dashboard does the watching, and the morning report tells each manager and parts lead what to focus on. The report is only as good as the data we put in — clock-ins, work orders, and silo tags. This guide is how we keep that data clean so the report stays honest.

The one rule that ties it all together: what you enter into the dashboard is the shop's numbers. A missing clock-out, a $0 work order, or an untagged part doesn't just look messy — it makes real revenue and labor invisible (or worse, lands it on the wrong department).

Time Clock — Techs & Tech Leads

Technicians

Your clock-ins ARE the shop's labor numbers. Five steps. The same steps, every day.

STEP 1EVERY MORNING — before you touch a tool
  • Check in to the RO via the Dashboard directly OR scan the QR code on the RV's key tag. (First time ever: sign in with your Patriots RV Google account. It remembers you after that.)
  • Tap the ONE RO Service you are about to work — Roof, Solar, Repairs, etc. Your pick decides which department gets your hours. Wrong pick = wrong books.
  • Tap Clock In / Start Work. Done. Go work.
STEP 2EVERY TIME you SWITCH RO jobs or RVs
  • At the OLD RV RO: open its check-in (dashboard or QR), tap Clock Out / Clock Out & Switch RO.
  • At the NEW RV RO: open its Tech check-in, pick the service, clock in. Clock In will also log you out of the last RO Service in case you didn't log out already.
STEP 3END OF EVERY JOB
  • Open the RV's check-in (dashboard or QR). Type what you did in Work Notes.
  • Tap Clock Out.
STEP 4END OF EVERY DAY — when you're done for the day
  • Ask yourself: am I clocked out? Not 100% sure? Open the check-in page and check.
  • If you forget, the system force-closes you at 5 PM and creates phantom hours — a forgotten 7:30 AM clock-in becomes a fake 9.5-hour day, flagged ⚠ on manager reports with YOUR name on it.
STEP 5TECH LEADS ONLY — when your team's RO service work is 100% finished
  • Clock out like normal (Step 3).
  • On the “Clocked Out!” screen a green OUR WORK IS DONE button appears — tap it.
  • That tells your manager the job is ready for final review. It does NOT close the job — your manager does the final ✓. If it says no work order exists, tell your manager.
Automatic: the RV's Status and the Work Order flip to In Progress when you clock in. Your only jobs: right RO and Service, Clock Out, and when the RO Service is complete, the Lead hits OUR WORK IS DONE.

Service Silo Managers

Roof / Solar / Vroom / Repair

P&L weeks run Monday through Sunday. Every number traces to an action below: Revenue = work-order dollar values, counted only when you click ✓ Mark Completed. Labor = tech clock-ins. Parts cost = wholesale × qty + freight, attributed by the part's Service Silo tag.

STEP 1THE MOMENT A JOB IS SCOPED — before work starts
  • Build the Work Order — real Dollar Value, right silo. The WO's Dollar Value IS the revenue number. No WO, or a $0 WO = the job is invisible to the P&L, no matter how much work goes into it.
  • Don't worry about status — the WO flips to In Progress by itself at the first tech clock-in.
STEP 2EVERY DAY — keep your techs' time clean
  • Right RO, right service at clock-in, clocked out at day end.
  • Forgotten clock-outs force-close at 5 PM and create ⚠ phantom hours — one forgotten morning clock-in = a fake 9.5-hour day on your silo's labor.
STEP 3WHEN THE BLUE “TECH DONE” CHIP APPEARS on a WO
  • Your tech lead tapped “Our work is done” at the kiosk. That is your cue: QA/QC the job and inform the customer.
STEP 4THE SAME WEEK THE WORK FINISHES — recognize the revenue
  • Click ✓ Mark Completed on the WO. This is THE revenue event. Until you click it, Revenue Completed stays $0 — no matter how finished the job is.
  • Revenue counts in the week you click — don't batch them up.
  • Clicked too early? ↩ Reopen pulls the revenue back out.

Parts Managers

Parts

Parts cost only lands on the right department when the part carries a Service Silo. Tag it and the math is automatic; leave it blank and it disappears into Unattributed.

STEP 1EVERY PART YOU ENTER — tag the silo
  • Set the Service Silo dropdown on every part — it marks the silos already “— on this RO” for you.
  • A blank silo sends the part to the Unattributed bucket — one June week had $19,068 sitting there that no department answered for.
STEP 2EVERY PART YOU ENTER — get the cost fields right
  • Wholesale cost is per unit (the system multiplies by quantity).
  • Freight goes in its own field. Together these are the parts-cost side of the P&L.
STEP 3EVERY TECH PARTS REQUEST — add the missing silo
  • Tech requests arrive without a silo today. Tag it when you process the request — match it to the RO's work order.
STEP 4ANY OLD PART YOU TOUCH — re-tag it
  • There is a backlog of untagged parts. Whenever you open one, set its silo before you move on.

Reading the Flags

Managers & Parts

When a report or the dashboard flags something, it points back at one of the steps above. Here's who clears each one.

FlagWhat it meansWho fixes it
Amber dot on a silo week Hours or parts spent but no completed revenue — a WO was never Mark-Completed, or has no Dollar Value. Silo manager
⚠ on calendar / timeline Auto-closed session at 5 PM = phantom hours. The tech forgot to clock out. Tech (mgr coaches)
Unattributed row Parts with no Service Silo tag. Parts manager
Blue “Tech done” chip Tech lead finished — job is waiting on manager QA/QC + Mark Completed. Silo manager
🔥 Fire Watch A promised date is near or overdue, or a part is past its ETA. Most likely to burn a customer if it slips. Silo manager
Idle RO On your work list with no activity 5+ business days. Get a tech on it, schedule it, set Awaiting Parts/Approval, or remove it. Silo manager
