## BUG bucket (front of the line)

| # | Submitter | Request | Mapped code (current) | Severity | Verdict |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| n40 | Sofia | Check-in no longer emails staff that a customer checked in, nor sends staff a copy of the authorization form | `customer-checkin.html` v1.9 submit handler | functional / small | Regression. Two gaps confirmed by reading the submit path. |

**n40 detail.** In `customer-checkin.html` the submit handler has two staff-facing
email paths:

1. **RAF copy** (`sendRAFEmail` -> `send-quote-email` `type:'raf_copy'`). The edge
   function CCs the staff group (`info@patriotsrvservices.com`, env
   `CUSTOMER_EMAIL_CC_GROUP`), but the call is wrapped in `if (email) { ... }`
   (~L2165) -- it only fires when the **customer** entered an email. A drop-off
   with no customer email sends **no RAF copy at all**, so staff get no
   authorization-form copy.
2. **Staff "checked-in" notification** (`sendLeadStaffNotification` -> `repair@patriotsrvservices.com`).
   It is gated by `shouldNotifyStaff = mode==='lead' || (mode==='dropoff' && (roType==='warranty' || roType==='hybrid'))`
   (~L2187). A **standard New Work drop-off** -- the common front-desk returning-customer
   case -- is neither a lead nor warranty/hybrid, so **no staff notice fires**.

Net effect matches Sofia's report exactly: standard check-ins/drop-offs no longer
reliably notify staff. **Recommended fix** (auto-fixable class, small):
extend `shouldNotifyStaff` to fire for all drop-offs (or add an always-on
"customer checked in" staff notice), and decouple the staff RAF CC from the
`if (email)` customer-email gate so staff always receive the authorization-form
copy. Confirm the intended notification scope with Roland before shipping (the
narrowing may have been deliberate for warranty triage).
